Charities we recommend!

We are just a newstarted project and so there are many wildcat charities that does very good work that you can read more about. Until we get setup we would recommend them if you want to make a donation.

Panthera

Founded in 2006, Panthera is devoted to the protection of wild cats such as tigers, lions and pumas. their ecosystems. Panthera teams up with cat biologists, international nongovernmental organizations, local communities, governments and ordinary citizens. One of Panthera's plans, Tigers Forevers hopes to increase the Asian tiger population in select locations by 50 percent in 10 years and calls for monitoring tigers to prevent poaching, among other strategies.

The Wildcat Sanctuary

Wildcat Sanctuary in Sandstone, Minn. provides a haven for wild cats in need and works to change Americans attitudes about these animals. Unfortunately, many people who buy big cats for the sake of entertainment find out quickly that they are unable to care properly for these animals and end up sending them to sanctuaries. TWS acts as a no-kill nonprofit that provides a refuge for the rest of the cat's life. This sanctuary, unlike others, is not open to the public..

Wildlife Conservation Society: Big Cats

Wildlife Big Cats is a subgroup of the Wildlife Conservation Society focused on saving wildlife and wild places through science, education and conservation and by inspiring individuals about the value of big cats. The main cats it focuses on are tigers and lions, because they have suffered the highest amount of losses due to illegal poaching. WCS believes that breeding helps with the preserving and recover process for endangered species.
